Must-See Attractions in Plaka

Plaka is home to many ancient monuments, including the Acropolis, the Ancient Agora, and the Roman Agora. You can also find Byzantine churches, Ottoman-era buildings, and neoclassical houses.

Getting to Plaka

Plaka is easily accessible by metro, bus, and taxi. The closest metro stations are Acropoli and Monastiraki. You can also take a bus to Syntagma Square and walk to Plaka.

General Tips for Tourists

  • Stay Hydrated: Especially during the hot summer months, drink plenty of water.
  • Wear Comfortable Shoes: Plaka has many cobblestone streets, so comfortable footwear is a must.
  • Be Aware of Pickpockets: Like in any tourist area, keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded places.
  • Respect Cultural Sites: When visiting churches or monasteries, dress modestly (shoulders and knees covered).
  • Learn Basic Greek Phrases: Locals appreciate it if you try a few words like "kalimera" (good morning) or "efharisto" (thank you).
  • Tipping: While not always mandatory, it's customary to round up the bill or leave a small tip (5-10%) for good service in restaurants and cafes.
  • Sun Protection: Use sunscreen, wear a hat, and sunglasses, particularly from spring to autumn.
